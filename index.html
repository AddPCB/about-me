This is my about me page.

My name is Peter Brightmore, and I am an experienced hospitality professional and aspiring software developer.
Throughout my decade-long hospitality career, I have shown a knack for problem solving and meeting targets without the need for oversight.
This enabled me to rise to the role of Head Chef, despite not having the relevant formal training or education.

As Head Chef, I successfully led a small team in turning around a struggling kitchen, and I was instrumental in implementing standards and protocols that resulted in a flawless 5* hygiene accreditation.
Along with my perfect attendance and punctuality, the project management skills I developed to deliver on due diligence, staff rotas, menu writing, and other office tasks made me a dependable asset, and will easily transfer to future roles.

Since the start of the pandemic, I have been looking to capitalize on my ADHD, rather than hide it as I did in hospitality.
To that end, I am in the process of transitioning into software development, and I am currently in the second week of a full stack JavaScript boot camp.
I have 7 GCEs A-C, and some college education. I have attempted to become a CISCO certified network associate, but my work commitments have previously prevented me from completing this course.
I am both a fast reader and typist, and I enjoy reading a variety of novels, news, and science journals.
When not reading, I can often be found debating advances in tech and current events.

My goals are to build a strong foundational knowledge of the tools, operators, and logic that form the basis of success in software development, and then become self-reliant in the industry by the second quarter of 2023.
Through this, I hope to make full use of my resourcefulness, creativity, and drive, which have been integral to my success in the hospitality industry.
